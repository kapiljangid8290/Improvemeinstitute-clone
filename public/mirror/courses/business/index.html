Business Studies Tutoring in Dubai

Business Studies is one of those subjects where students often feel comfortable until the exam, and then discover that comfort and marks are not the same thing. Case study questions, evaluation tasks, and data response questions all require a specific kind of thinking — and specific technique — that is rarely given enough focus in school. At Improve ME, Business Studies is taught from GCSE and IGCSE through to A-Level and IB Diploma, with a focus on the question types that actually appear in papers.

Business Studies Is Not Common Sense

The most damaging misconception about Business Studies is that it requires no real preparation — that it is 'common sense' compared to the sciences or mathematics. Students who arrive at GCSE or A-Level with this assumption consistently underperform. Business Studies rewards students who know exactly how to structure an answer for their specific exam board, how to apply theory to case study material, and how to write evaluatively. These are learned skills. They do not come from reading the textbook.

How Top Students Answer Business Studies Questions

At GCSE, IGCSE, and A-Level, our students use the PEEL method for extended answers: Point, Explain, Example, Link back to context. For evaluation questions, we use the AJIM structure: Answer, Justify, It Depends On, Most Importantly. These frameworks ensure students hit every assessment objective the mark scheme rewards — consistently. Each exam board has a different paper structure and question style. AQA, Edexcel, Cambridge International, and OCR all require board-specific practice materials. Using the wrong past papers — or mixed papers — is one of the most common preparation mistakes we see.

Separate Sessions for Every Specification

Business Studies and Economics are subjects where exam board matters enormously — AQA, Edexcel, Cambridge International, and OCR all have different paper structures, question styles, and marking priorities. At Improve ME, we run separate sessions for each specification and year group throughout the day. Your child is never in a mixed-spec group where preparation for the wrong board becomes someone else's problem.

Is Your Child Ready for GCSE Business Studies?

Students should be comfortable with percentages, ratios, graphs, averages, and basic algebra before beginning GCSE or IGCSE Business Studies. These skills appear frequently in calculations — elasticity, break-even analysis, national income, and inflation all require numerical fluency. Students who are not confident with these areas will benefit from additional maths support alongside their Business Studies preparation.
