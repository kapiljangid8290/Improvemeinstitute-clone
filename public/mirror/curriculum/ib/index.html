PARENT GUIDE

What is the IB Diploma? A Parent's Guide

Everything you need to know about the most demanding pre-university programme

The International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme (IBDP) is a rigorous two-year qualification for students aged 16–18, recognised by top universities worldwide as one of the most demanding and respected pre-university programmes available.

Students study 6 subjects across different subject groups — 3 at Higher Level (HL) and 3 at Standard Level (SL) — alongside three core components: Theory of Knowledge (TOK), the Extended Essay (EE), and Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS).

The maximum score is 45 points. Most competitive universities require 38+ for selective courses. Our IB teaching targets scores of 6 and 7 — the bands that open doors to competitive university programmes worldwide. At Improve ME, our IB tutors understand not just the content but the specific Internal Assessment requirements, mark schemes, and examiner expectations that make the difference between a 5 and a 7.

IB CORE

The Three Core Components

Theory of Knowledge (TOK)

A course that explores the nature of knowledge and how we know what we know. Assessed through an essay and a presentation, with up to 3 bonus points contributing to the total Diploma score.

Extended Essay (EE)

An independent, self-directed piece of research culminating in a 4,000-word essay. Students choose a topic from one of their subjects (or an approved area). Success can add up to 3 points to the Diploma total.

Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS)

A programme of experiential learning outside the classroom. Students complete projects and reflections across creativity, physical activity, and service. CAS is not graded but must be completed to receive the Diploma.
