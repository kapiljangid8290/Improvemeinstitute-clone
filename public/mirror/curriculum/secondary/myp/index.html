PARENT GUIDE

What is MYP? A Parent's Guide

Understanding the IB Middle Years Programme and how we support it

The IB Middle Years Programme (MYP) is a challenging 5-year framework for students aged 11–16, designed by the International Baccalaureate. It bridges the IB Primary Years Programme (PYP) and the IB Diploma Programme (DP), developing internationally minded, critical thinkers.

MYP goes beyond memorisation — it focuses on inquiry-based learning, interdisciplinary thinking, and real-world problem solving. Students are assessed through internal tasks, a Personal Project, and eAssessments.

At Improve ME, our tutors understand the MYP framework deeply — from subject-specific criteria to the ATL (Approaches to Learning) skills that underpin MYP success. We target criterion scores of 6 and 7 across all assessed criteria.

WHY IT MATTERS

Why MYP Matters More Than Ever

The MYP is increasingly recognised by top schools and universities as rigorous preparation for the IB Diploma. Students who excel in MYP enter the DP with stronger inquiry skills, better essay-writing habits, and deeper subject knowledge.

In Dubai's competitive school environment — with IB World Schools like GEMS, Jumeirah College, and Dubai International Academy — strong MYP results are critical for progression into the Diploma Programme.
