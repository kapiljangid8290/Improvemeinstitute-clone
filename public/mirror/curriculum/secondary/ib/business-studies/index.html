PARENT GUIDE

What is IB Business Studies? A Parent's Guide

Understanding the subject, the assessment, and what strong results actually require

IB Business Studies develops students' ability to apply business management theories and tools to real-world contexts. Whether studying at Standard Level or Higher Level, our tutor helps students build the analytical and evaluative writing skills that examiners reward — from structured responses to case study analysis.

The subject is assessed through a combination of written examinations and an Internal Assessment (IA). At Higher Level, students also complete an additional paper and extended case study analysis. At Standard Level, the focus is on core concepts and structured responses across two papers.

What makes IB Business Studies genuinely challenging is the level of analytical depth required. It is not enough to describe a concept — examiners expect students to evaluate, apply real business tools, and construct well-reasoned arguments. Our tutors teach the difference between a response that describes and a response that earns a 7.

WHY IT MATTERS

Why IB Business Studies Results Matter

IB Business Studies is a popular subject at leading Dubai IB schools including GEMS World Academy, Jumeirah College, and Dubai International Academy. Cohorts are large and competition is strong — meaning the difference between a 5 and a 7 often comes down to exam technique and IA quality, not knowledge alone.

Students at Dubai schools following the IB Diploma Programme need structured support to move from surface-level answers to the higher-order analytical responses needed for Grades 5, 6, and 7. Strong results open direct pathways to Business, Economics, Finance, and Management programmes at top universities.

The IA makes or breaks borderline grades

The Internal Assessment contributes 25% of the final grade. A well-executed IA can lift a student from a 5 to a 6 — or from a 6 to a 7. We guide students from research question selection through to the final submission.

Command terms determine exam marks

IB examiners reward precision. 'Examine', 'evaluate', and 'justify' each require a different type of response. We train students to identify command terms instantly and structure their answers accordingly.

METHODOLOGY

How We Prepare Students for IB Business Studies

Structured, examiner-focused preparation from Year 1 through to final papers

IB Business Studies rewards students who can think analytically, apply business tools accurately, and write with precision under exam conditions. Our preparation is built around exactly those skills.

01

Small Groups (Max 6 Students)

All sessions run in small groups of no more than 6 students, with separate sessions structured around HL and SL syllabus content.

This keeps teaching focused and allows the tutor to tailor explanations to the level each student is sitting — without HL-only content distracting SL students, or vice versa.

02

Command Term Focus

Heavy focus on the command terms IB examiners use — Explain, Analyse, Evaluate, Discuss — and how each demands a different type of response.

Most students know the content. The ones who score 7s know how to deploy it. We close that gap.

03

Past Paper Practice with Structured Feedback

We work through past papers using real IB mark schemes so students understand exactly what earns marks and why.

Timed practice, structured feedback, and mark scheme comparison are built into every stage of preparation — from early revision through to final exam readiness.

04

Case Study Technique

We teach students to apply business tools — SWOT, Ansoff Matrix, and others — in exam answers rather than just describing them.

IB examiners reward application and evaluation, not description. We train students to read an unseen case study, identify the key issue, select the right framework, and construct an evaluative response under time pressure.
