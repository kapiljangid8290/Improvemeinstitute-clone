OVERVIEW

What is A-Level Business?

A-Level Business Studies develops analytical, evaluative, and strategic thinking through the study of how real businesses operate, make decisions, and respond to change. It covers marketing, finance, operations, human resources, and strategic management, combining quantitative calculations with extended evaluative writing. A-Level Business is highly valued by universities for Business, Economics, Management, and Finance degree programmes. In Dubai's international schools, the subject is examined through AQA, Edexcel, and OCR — all of which place significant weight on case study analysis, data interpretation, and the ability to construct and justify a well-reasoned argument. At Improve ME, our Business Studies tutors build the analytical frameworks and evaluation skills that distinguish A and A* candidates.

COURSE CONTENT

A-Level Business Course Content

Advanced depth with step-by-step problem-solving, synoptic assessment preparation, and top-grade strategy for university.

Business Decisions & Financial Management

  • •Business objectives — profit, growth, market share, social enterprise, stakeholder conflict
  • •Decision making — scientific decision making, decision trees, expected values, investment appraisal (NPV, IRR, payback)
  • •Financial statements — income statements, balance sheets, cash flow statements (construction and interpretation)
  • •Ratio analysis — profitability, liquidity, efficiency, gearing ratios (calculation, interpretation, and limitations)
  • •Sources of finance — internal, external, short and long-term (advantages and limitations)
  • •Financial planning — break-even analysis, budgeting, variance analysis, working capital management
  • •Business strategy — Ansoff Matrix, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT analysis, strategic direction and competitive advantage

Marketing, People & Operations

  • •Market research — primary and secondary, quantitative and qualitative, sampling methods
  • •Marketing strategy — segmentation, targeting, positioning, the marketing mix (7Ps), digital marketing
  • •Product — product life cycle, Boston Matrix, new product development, branding
  • •Pricing strategies — cost-plus, penetration, skimming, predatory, psychological pricing
  • •Human resource management — recruitment and selection, training and development, motivation theories (Taylor, Maslow, Herzberg, McClelland)
  • •Organisational structure — hierarchies, spans of control, delegation, centralisation vs decentralisation, matrix structures
  • •Operations management — lean production, JIT, TQM, capacity utilisation, productivity, quality management

The External Environment & Evaluation Skills

  • •Economic environment — business cycle, inflation, interest rates, exchange rates (impact on business decisions)
  • •Globalisation — multinational corporations, offshoring, trade barriers, ethical trading, emerging markets
  • •Technology — impact of digital technology, e-commerce, automation, big data, artificial intelligence on business
  • •Ethics and corporate social responsibility — stakeholder theory, ethical trading, sustainability, triple bottom line
  • •Legal environment — employment law, consumer protection, competition law (impact on business strategy)
  • •Evaluation and judgement — constructing a justified conclusion, weighing up arguments, considering context, avoiding assertion
ASSESSMENT

Assessment Structure

Assessment includes data response and essay papers, with synoptic elements at A-Level. We build evaluation and application to context for university prep.

Paper 1
Business Decisions and Marketing
  • •2 hours, 100 marks — 33%
  • •Data response and essay questions on themes 1 and 2
  • •25-mark essay questions requiring evaluation, justification, and supported conclusion
Paper 2
Business Activities, Decisions, and Strategy
  • •2 hours, 100 marks — 33%
  • •Data response and essay questions on themes 3 and 4
  • •25-mark essays; quantitative skills (investment appraisal, ratio analysis, break-even)
Paper 3
Integrated Business
  • •2 hours, 100 marks — 33%
  • •Pre-released case study on a real or realistic business; all questions based on the case study
  • •All three papers include 25-mark essay questions requiring evaluation and justified conclusion

TEACHING APPROACH

Our A-Level Business Teaching Approach

01

Case Study Analysis Technique

The Paper 3 pre-released case study is one of the most important elements of A-Level Business. We work through the case study in depth, identifying the key business issues, financial data, and strategic context — so students enter the exam with confident, informed answers rather than generic ones.

02

25-Mark Essay Framework

25-mark essays account for a significant proportion of total marks across all three papers and require a specific structure — analysis of both sides, use of evidence and context, and a justified conclusion. We teach and practise this framework until students can produce a high-quality 25-mark response reliably within 35 minutes.

03

Quantitative Skills

A-Level Business includes a significant quantitative component — investment appraisal, ratio analysis, break-even, and financial statement construction. We ensure students are confident with all calculation types and can interpret results in a business context.

04

Evaluation and Judgement

The highest marks in Business Studies are reserved for genuine evaluation — considering context, weighing competing arguments, and drawing justified conclusions. We develop this skill deliberately in every session rather than treating it as something students either have or do not.
