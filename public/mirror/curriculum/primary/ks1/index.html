Curriculum & Assessment

GL Assessments and teacher assessment at the end of KS1

At the end of Year 2, children in England (and many British curriculum schools in Dubai) are assessed in Reading, Mathematics, and sometimes Grammar, Punctuation and Spelling. These are often referred to as GL Assessments and year-group progress checks. Results are usually based on a combination of tests and teacher assessment.

At Improve ME, we don't teach to the test alone — we build solid understanding and confidence so that when assessment time comes, your child is prepared. We can also offer targeted practice and familiarisation with the format so children know what to expect.

About This Stage

KS1 covers Year 1 and Year 2, typically ages 5 to 7.
We offer Mathematics and English at KS1 level, both following our structured MaThesis and EngLit programmes.
Yes. The foundational skills developed at this age — number fluency, reading comprehension, writing mechanics — are universal. Our programmes build these core skills regardless of which curriculum your child's school follows.

Our Sessions

One teacher to two students. Most sessions run with one or two children — never more.
Once or twice a week is typical for KS1. We recommend starting with one session per week and reviewing after the first month.

Subjects & Curriculum

We align with KS1 curriculum frameworks but teach through our own structured MaThesis and EngLit programmes. Sessions support what your child is learning in school while building deeper understanding that classroom time alone cannot always provide.
We start with a baseline assessment to identify exactly where your child is. From there we address gaps systematically — your child catches up, builds confidence, and moves forward without the pressure of a classroom setting.

Progress & Results

We track what your child has covered and how confidently they have grasped it every session. Parents receive regular honest updates — not vague reassurances.

Getting Started

The earlier the better. KS1 is where core arithmetic and literacy habits form. Addressing gaps now is significantly easier than trying to fill them at KS2 or beyond.
